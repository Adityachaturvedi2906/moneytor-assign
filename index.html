FAQs

What types of debt can Moneytor care help me with? ⌄

most of your unsecured debts (not backed by a collateral) can be enrolled. If the majority of your debt load consists of personal loans, credit card debts, consumer loans, payday loans, peer-to-peer loans, BNPL loans, and education loans; you can expect a good result from our Debt Relief Platform.

What is Debt Settlement? ⌄

debt settlement involves negotiating with creditors to reduce debt amounts or establish structured repayment plans, helping you regain control of your finances.

how does settlement affect my credit score? ⌄

settlement may harm your credit score since it involves paying less than the full debt, leading to a 'partial payment' notation on your credit report.

what is NPA (Non Performing Asset)? ⌄

NPA (Non Performing Asset) denotes loans or credits that are in default, resulting in no interest or principal payments for a specific duration.

what is FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio)? ⌄

FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) is a lender's tool to evaluate your ability to repay loans, comparing your fixed obligations, like loan payments, to your income.

what is the difference between Collection and Counseling? ⌄

collection refers to creditors' efforts to collect overdue payments, while counseling provides financial guidance for effective debt management.

how to build Debt Settlement? ⌄

building debt settlement involves partnering with a professional firm, creating a budget, and saving to negotiate settlements with creditors.

how to stop Creditors’ calls? ⌄

to stop creditor calls, you can request written communication, express your intent to work on a repayment plan, or explore legal options like bankruptcy protection.

how much does Debt Settlement cost? ⌄

debt settlement costs encompass fees to settlement companies and the negotiated settlement amounts with creditors, varying based on your circumstances.

what is a good Credit Score? ⌄

a good credit score is typically considered as 700 or higher on the FICO scale, though the definition may vary depending on the lender and credit type sought.
